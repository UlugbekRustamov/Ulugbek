If we don’t act now, tomorrow it’s
consequences will reach all of us

For centuries Aral Sea was the world’s fourth largest inland lake,
offering its treasures and sustaining many settlements, where
people enjoyed prosperous lives as fishermen, farmers, herders,
craftsmen and traders.

However, in just the past 60 to 80 years, the sea lost 90% of its
size and was transformed into the Aralkum desert. This disaster
has had catastrophic impacts on biodiversity with many types of
fish, animals and plants completely disappearing from the
region.
Toxic salts and sand are picked up by winds and carried out over
to hundreds and thousands of kilometers harming people’s
livelihoods and well being and impacting their identities and
culture.
Studies show that each year, winds from Aral Sea carry 100
million tons of toxic sand and salts that reach out European
cities, glaciers of Island, forests in Norway and even Antarctica.

Transforming the tragedy into
the new opportunities for all

There is a consensus that bring water back to the
Larger South Aral Sea is unlikely.
However, it does not mean nothing can be done.
You can make a difference for people living there and
beyond by making the environment healthier and more
suitable.
We aim to plant Saxaul in the Aral seabed and
transform 100 hectares into forest.
Saxaul can help stopping the spread of toxic salts and
desertification – just one tree can fix and hold up to 4
tons of sand.
This will be a FIRST but CRUCIAL MOVE to cover Aral Sea
with green blanket, helping protect the environment
and give people hope for a brighter future.

Your gift is any investmet today to
Make a real change

Your investment means a real action to:
• Give people hope for a brighter future
• Mitigate climate change and CO2 emissions
• Prevent the further spread of toxic salts and
sand across Central Asia and beyond
• Help transform the sea bed/desert into a
green blanket of trees
• Improve biodiversity and have a positive
impact on the environment
• Create and support sustainable livelihood
opportunities and improve health conditions for
people in the Aral Sea region

HOW CAN YOU CONTRIBUTE:

We have a dedicate land plot in the seabed of Aral Sea of
first 100 hectares (247 acres), where saxaul will be planted.

$1 plants a saxaul. Saxaul is ‘a salt cedar’ and one of the
most important and useful native plants in the arid region.
